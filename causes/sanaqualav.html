Open defecation has become a common phenomenon in developing and underdeveloped nations, especially in Nigeria. Open defecation is the riskiest sanitation practice WHO/UNICEF (2016). In 2018 World Health Organization and UNICEF revealed that about 2.3 billion people around the world donâ€™t have access to safe sanitation, and practice open defecation instead of using toilets connected to sewer lines. In addition to this, as at 2018, over 108 million Nigerians lacking accessible clean toilets (United Nations), 58 million people do not have access to clean water, and over 55,000 children under 5 die as a result of poor water and sanitation (Water Aid). In order to meet the Sustainable Development Goal (SDGs) 6 on water, sanitation and sustainable hygiene (WASH), there is an urgent need for an action plan to crop these numbers.

Achieving the UN Sustainable Development Goal 6 by 2030 requires extraordinary efforts. Based on World Bank estimates, Nigeria will be required to triple its budget or at least allocate 1.7 percent of the current Gross Domestic Product to WASH.

At INADES, we aim to revolutionize the provision of free public toilet facilities, which will serve more than being just a toilet but a cell site / hub.

The following added facilities and services will be in the public toilets.

  • Solar Panel: for generation of electricity in order to ensure provision of 24hrs constant electricity.
  • First Aid Kit: to provide the collection of supplies and first aid equipments to the public.
  • Information Centre: A medium to pass information relevant to the public.
  • Internet Service: Access to internet for the public for a limited time daily.
  • Charging points: a secured and well structured charging point for the public to charge their mobile phone.
  • Storage: To keep equipment for maintenance.
  • Green Sitting Area
